About Blog Work

Chiara is a nineteen-year-old from Manila. She's currently a sophomore at Yale studying computer science & visual design. Since I was young, all I've ever wanted to do is create. Here's me on paper.

A core part of me lives on in creation through experiences and stories on the web, from coding and designing in computer shops to exploring the browser as a narrative medium. I want to rediscover the best ways to bring human creation and community at the forefront of all we do.

Currently, I run Developh where we nurture student founders in Southeast Asia while launching mission-first products to reshift the tech pipeline, User Experience Society at Yale, and Technology.ph, a biweekly newsletter on SEA tech and innovation. I also do creative research at the Yale CCAM, design for sociopolitical initiatives, branded Southeast Asia's largest Pride Fest, worked amongst lit journals + magazines.

Most of the time I'm building web experiences, games, or writing. My archives, itch.io, and github host a lot of those.

As an occasional speaker, I was the 2018 AnitaB.org Student of Vision at the GHC keynote, and have spoken in workshops of 20 to audiences of 20,000.

Other times, I love attending shows, cartography, building indie games (especially on the browser), music journalism (~140,000 minutes of Spotify a year—missing my YT + Bandcamp + local collections), collecting vinyl, game design & tabletop RPGs, worldbuilding, and coming-of-age movies (current tops: Boyhood, Himizu, Manila In The Claws of Light). I am also looking for a D&D group.
